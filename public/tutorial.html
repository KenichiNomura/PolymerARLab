Tutorial

Polymer AR Lab

Turn a hand-drawn Lewis structure — or any molecule from PubChem — into an interactive 3-D model, view it in augmented reality on the desk in front of you, build polymer chains, and export ready-to-run LAMMPS input for a quick relaxation.

🖥️ Desktop and 📱 mobile both work. On a phone you scan with the live camera; on a computer you upload a photo. Everything runs in your browser — nothing to install.

Interface tour ① Scan a sketch → AR ② PubChem → polymer → LAMMPS Tips

The interface at a glance

The 3-D molecule fills the screen. Controls sit in the corners: a row of tool icons at the top-left, the panel toggles and save button at the bottom-left, and the immersive-AR button at the top-right.

Top-left tool icons

CameraTurn the live camera on/off to scan a drawing (mainly for phones). Lights up when active. Once it's on, tap the on-screen frame to capture (drag its edges to resize, drag it to move).
Reset viewRecenter the camera on the molecule if you've orbited or zoomed away.
ClearRemove the current molecule and start from a blank scene.
AR ViewOn iPhone/iPad, open the molecule in AR Quick Look to place it on a real surface. Glows amber when the AR model is ready. (On desktop it downloads a .usdz file.)

Bottom-left panel toggles

EditOpen/close the molecule panel: upload a sketch, load from PubChem, toggle labels/hydrogens.
Polymer builderOpen/close the polymer panel: choose the curing mechanism (addition or condensation), load your monomer(s), pick anchor atoms, and grow the chain (see Walkthrough ②). Opening it turns on atom labels automatically.
StatusShow/hide a small panel with the molecule summary (atoms, bonds, bond orders), the latest message, and a valence check.
TutorialOpen this tutorial page.
Save LAMMPS (UFF)Download the current structure as a LAMMPS data file + run script (see Walkthrough ②).

Inside the Edit panel

What each control does

Upload sketch — pick a photo of a Lewis structure (best on desktop).

PubChem — type a name (e.g. caffeine) or a CID (e.g. 2244) and press Load to fetch the real 3-D structure.

Atom labels — show each atom's element + index (C1, C2, O1…). You use these labels to pick polymer anchors.

Show hydrogens — display/hide H atoms (on by default; keep it on before saving LAMMPS files).

Building polymers lives in its own panel now — tap the chain icon in the dock (Walkthrough ②).

Inside the Polymer panel

How the builder works

Choose the mechanism first — the panel opens with two big buttons: Addition cure (opens a C=C) or Condensation cure (each bond releases a small molecule — H₂O, or HCl from acyl chlorides). Choosing one starts from a clean scene.

A / B slots — condensation polymers can use one self-condensing monomer (slot A only) or two different monomers. Tap a slot, then load a molecule into it; with both filled, they appear together labelled A and B.

Anchors follow the highlighted slot — the dropdowns always pick atoms of the active slot's monomer. Wrong picks show an error explaining what can react.

✕ Start over — back to the mechanism chooser (asks first if a chain is showing).

① Scan a hand-drawn Lewis structure & view it in AR

Draw a molecule on paper, let the app recognize it into 3-D, then stand it up on your desk in AR.

  1. 1
    Draw a Lewis structure on paper — clear bond lines and element letters recognize best (e.g. water, CO₂, ethanol).
  2. 2
    Capture it. 📱 Phone: tap the Camera icon, position the drawing inside the on-screen frame (drag its edges to resize), then tap the frame to capture. A shutter flash and a "Recognizing…" spinner show while it works. 🖥️ Desktop: tap Edit → Upload sketch and choose a photo. The app recognizes the drawing and builds the 3-D molecule.
  3. 3
    Explore. Drag to orbit, scroll/pinch to zoom, Reset view to recenter. Open Edit to toggle Atom labels and Show hydrogens; open Status to see atom/bond counts. Clear starts over.
  4. 4
    See it in AR — the molecule stands on your paper.
    📱 iPhone / iPad: tap the AR View icon (top-left, the cube). Wait for it to glow amber, tap again to open AR Quick Look, then aim at your paper and place it.
    🤖 Android: tap START AR (top-right), aim at your paper on the desk, and tap the screen — the molecule appears on that spot.
AR needs a rear camera and a secure (https://) page. If AR isn't available on your device, you'll still get the full interactive 3-D view.

② Download a monomer, build a polymer, export LAMMPS

Fetch a monomer from PubChem, tell the app which two atoms form the backbone, tile it into a chain, and download a ready-to-run LAMMPS relaxation.

  1. 1
    Open the Polymer builder. Tap the chain icon in the bottom-left dock and choose how your polymer cures: Addition cure (opens a C=C double bond) or Condensation cure (each new bond releases one small molecule — H₂O or HCl). The scene clears so you start fresh.
  2. 2
    Load a monomer. Type a name or CID in the panel's PubChem box (try ethylene or styrene for addition, lactic acid for condensation) and press Load. Atom labels (C1, C2, O1…) turn on automatically when the builder opens, so every atom shows its name.
  3. 3
    Pick the two anchor atoms. Anchor 1 and Anchor 2 are the atoms where the chain will connect — for a vinyl monomer the two C=C carbons; for condensation an acid carbon (−COOH, or −COCl on an acyl chloride) and the −OH oxygen (or −NH₂ nitrogen). The app suggests them, but the choice is yours — a wrong pick shows an error explaining what can react. Press Make repeat unit to form the chain. In condensation, every new link squeezes out a little water molecule that drifts away from the chain (the Status panel counts them).
  4. 4
    Two-monomer condensation (PET, nylon). Load ethylene glycol into slot A and pick its two −OH oxygens. Tap slot B, load terephthalic acid — both molecules appear together, labelled A and B. Pick B's two −COOH carbons and press Make repeat unit (combine A + B) — that's PET, the plastic in drink bottles. (hexamethylenediamine + adipic acid gives nylon 6,6 releasing water; swap in adipoyl chloride for the classic “nylon rope trick” chemistry, where every new bond releases HCl instead.) The released molecules stay a teaching visual — they are not included in AR Quick Look or LAMMPS exports.
  5. 5
    Set the chain length with the Repeats slider.
  6. 6
    Export for LAMMPS. Press the Save LAMMPS (UFF) download icon in the bottom-left dock. Two files download:
    <name>.data — atoms, bonds, and a self-contained UFF-style force field (no external force-field file needed).
    in.relax — the run script.
  7. 7
    Relax it. Run lmp -in in.relax. The script does an overlap-relief warm-up → FIRE energy minimization → NVT at 300 K for 10 ps, and writes:
    <name>.min.xyz (minimization trajectory), <name>.nvt.xyz (NVT trajectory), and <name>.relaxed.data (final structure).
Keep Show hydrogens on before saving — the export includes every hydrogen and caps the chain ends, so the polymer is a complete molecule LAMMPS can relax. The force field is generic (UFF-style, approximate) — great for getting a reasonable geometry, not for precise energetics.

Tips & troubleshooting

Scanning

Good light and dark, clear pen strokes help. Recognition is a best guess — check the Status panel's summary and valence note, and redraw if it's off.

AR

Needs a rear camera and an https:// page. iPhone/iPad use the AR View icon (Quick Look); Android uses START AR (WebXR). Grant camera permission when asked.

Reset vs Clear

Reset view only recenters the camera. Clear removes the molecule entirely.

Status panel

Tap the Status (info) icon for atom/bond counts, bond-order breakdown, and a valence check that flags unusual structures.
